About

I work on the part of machine learning that decides whether a system can be trusted: evaluation, grounding, and robustness — especially where the stakes are high, like healthcare and life sciences.

My background runs from computer-vision research on medical imaging (published at IEEE ICRITO) to LLM applications, retrieval, and AI-safety analysis. I’m most interested in the unglamorous work that happens after a model trains: figuring out where it fails, and how you’d know.

This site is where I write about what I’m building, what I’m reading, and where I think applied AI is heading.
